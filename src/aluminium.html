Aluminium Facts

  • Aluminium is the most abundant metal in the Earth's crust, making up about 8% of the crust by weight.
  • It was once considered more precious than gold and silver because it was so difficult to extract.
  • Aluminium is 100% recyclable and retains all its properties no matter how many times it's recycled.
  • Recycling aluminium requires only 5% of the energy needed to produce new aluminium from ore.
  • The top of the Washington Monument is capped with a 100-ounce pyramid of aluminium.
  • Aluminium is an excellent conductor of electricity and is often used in electrical transmission lines.
  • Despite being strong, aluminium is about one-third the density of steel.
  • Aluminium doesn't rust because it forms a protective oxide coating when exposed to air.
  • About 75% of all aluminium ever produced is still in use today due to recycling.
  • Aluminium foil was first used for packaging in 1911 for wrapping chocolate bars.
  • The chemical symbol for aluminium is Al and its atomic number is 13.
  • China is the world's largest producer of aluminium, accounting for over half of global production.

Aluminium Price Analysis (2015-2025)

Major Trends

  • Historic Bull Run (2021-2022): Explosive rally from $1,977 to $3,491 all-time high (+77%).
  • Current Consolidation: Stabilized at $2,400-$2,600 range after 2022 peak.
  • Regime Changes: Transition from range-bound (2015-2020) to crisis-driven (2021-2022) to new equilibrium (2025+).
  • Higher Price Floor: Established new base around $2,200 vs pre-2020 $1,600 levels.

Price Anomalies

  • March 2022 Peak: $3,491 all-time high - culmination of 12-month bull market.
  • Extreme Volatility: 2022 saw +16.5% surges and -12.6% crashes within months.
  • Volatility Clusters: Multiple 5-8% monthly moves in 2018 and renewed volatility in 2024.
  • Sharp Declines: April 2022 (-12.6%), March 2024 (-10.3%), July 2024 (-9.3%).

Potential Causes

  • Supply-Side Shocks: Energy-intensive production sensitive to electricity prices and geopolitical events.
  • Post-COVID Demand: Industrial recovery combined with supply chain disruptions.
  • Green Energy Transition: Growing demand from EVs and renewable infrastructure.
  • European Energy Crisis: 2022 Russia-Ukraine conflict impacting major production regions.

Future Outlook

  • New Trading Range: $2,200-$2,800 likely represents normalized trading band.
  • Elevated Volatility: Continued sensitivity to global industrial sentiment and energy prices.
  • Breakout Requirements: Significant fundamental catalysts needed to break current range.
  • Structural Demand: Green energy applications creating long-term support above historical levels.
