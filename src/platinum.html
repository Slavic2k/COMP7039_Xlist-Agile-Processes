Platinum Facts

  • Platinum was used by ancient Egyptians and pre-Columbian Americans over 3,000 years ago.
  • It is one of the rarest elements in Earth's crust, with annual production of only about 160 tons.
  • Platinum is extremely dense - a 6-inch cube of platinum would weigh about 165 pounds (75 kg).
  • It has a very high melting point of 1,768°C (3,214°F), higher than gold or silver.
  • About 50% of platinum production is used in catalytic converters for vehicles.
  • Platinum is highly resistant to corrosion and does not tarnish in air.
  • South Africa produces about 80% of the world's platinum, with Russia being the second-largest producer.
  • The name "platinum" comes from the Spanish word "platina" meaning "little silver".
  • Platinum is used in laboratory equipment, electrical contacts, and jewelry.
  • It is used in chemotherapy drugs to treat various types of cancer.
  • Platinum is 30 times rarer than gold, contributing to its high value.
  • The metal is also used in fuel cells and as a catalyst in the production of fertilizers.

Platinum Price Analysis (2015-2025)

Major Trends

  • Sustained Downtrend Reversal: Powerful recovery from 2020 lows to current multi-year highs.
  • Extended Basing Pattern: Five-year consolidation between $730-$1,200 from 2019-2024.
  • Explosive 2025 Breakout: Strong 64% surge from $910 to $1,489 in nine months.
  • Overall Performance: +50% increase from $990 (2015) to $1,489 (2025).

Price Anomalies

  • March 2020 COVID Crash: $730 cycle low representing 40% decline from early 2020 highs.
  • 2025 Breakout Acceleration: Consistent upward pressure throughout 2025 with strong momentum.
  • August 2022 Secondary Low: $832 level tested and held as support during market uncertainty.
  • December 2024 Base Completion: $910 established as final basing low before explosive rally.
  • Current Multi-Year High: $1,489 represents highest price level since 2016.

Potential Causes

  • Palladium Substitution: Auto manufacturers switching from expensive palladium creating new demand source.
  • Green Hydrogen Demand: Platinum's use in electrolyzers for hydrogen economy driving structural demand.
  • South African Supply Challenges: Production issues in dominant producing region supporting prices.
  • Investment Rediscovery: Renewed institutional interest after years of market neglect.
  • Supply Discipline: Producers maintaining capital discipline despite higher price environment.

Future Outlook

  • Bullish Trend Confirmation: Sustained move above $1,500 would confirm new bull market phase.
  • Measured Move Projection: $1,700-$1,800 target zone based on size of preceding base.
  • Critical Support Level: $1,200-$1,300 zone now represents key support after resistance break.
  • Fundamental Improvement: Supply-demand balance enhancement supporting higher price floor.
  • Regime Change Evidence: Combination of technical breakout and improving fundamentals suggesting sustained recovery.
