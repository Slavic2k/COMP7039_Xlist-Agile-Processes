Copper Facts

  • Copper was one of the first metals ever used by humans, dating back over 10,000 years.
  • It is an excellent conductor of electricity, second only to silver among common metals.
  • The Statue of Liberty contains over 179,000 pounds (81,300 kg) of copper.
  • Copper has natural antibacterial properties and is used in hospitals to reduce infections.
  • Chile is the world's largest producer of copper, accounting for about 28% of global production.
  • Copper is 100% recyclable and can be reused repeatedly without losing its properties.
  • The average home contains about 400 pounds (180 kg) of copper in wiring and plumbing.
  • Copper's distinctive reddish-orange color makes it one of the few colored metals.
  • The chemical symbol for copper is Cu, from the Latin word "cuprum" meaning "from Cyprus".
  • Copper is essential for human health and is found in many enzymes and proteins.
  • About 65% of all copper ever mined is still in use today due to recycling.
  • Copper turns green when exposed to air and moisture, forming a protective layer called patina.

Copper Price Analysis (2015-2025)

Major Trends

  • Transformational Bull Market: Copper demonstrated explosive growth from 2020 lows to all-time highs.
  • Sustained High Levels: Prices consolidated at historically elevated levels between $8,500-$10,000 since 2023.
  • Strong Long-term Uptrend: Higher highs and higher lows established consistently since 2016.
  • Overall Performance: +95% increase from $5,112 (2015) to $9,984 (2025).

Price Anomalies

  • March 2022 Record High: $10,375 all-time peak driven by green energy demand and supply constraints.
  • May 2021 Near-Record: $10,258 surge fueled by massive institutional positioning in green transition metals.
  • March 2020 COVID Crash: $4,951 low due to global industrial shutdowns and demand collapse.
  • July 2022 Major Correction: -23% decline from peak as recession fears overwhelmed supply concerns.
  • Recent Range Compression: Tight trading between $8,500-$10,000 suggesting energy buildup for next major move.

Potential Causes

  • Green Energy Transition: Massive copper demand from EVs, renewables, and grid infrastructure driving structural repricing.
  • Supply Constraints: Limited new major mine developments and declining ore grades creating persistent market tightness.
  • Chinese Demand Dominance: World's largest copper consumer driving global price patterns and inventory cycles.
  • Infrastructure Spending: Global construction cycles and government stimulus programs supporting demand.
  • Financialization: Increased institutional investment recognizing copper's strategic importance in energy transition.

Future Outlook

  • Critical Resistance Test: Current $10,000 level represents major psychological and technical barrier.
  • Breakout Potential: Successful move above $10,500 could trigger explosive next leg higher.
  • Strong Support Base: $8,000-$8,500 zone provides solid foundation after multiple successful tests.
  • Structural Demand Growth: Green energy applications ensuring long-term upward pressure on prices.
  • Supply Limitations: Persistent production challenges supporting elevated price floor compared to historical norms.
