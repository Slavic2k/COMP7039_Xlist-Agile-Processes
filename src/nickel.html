Nickel Facts

  • Nickel was discovered in 1751 by Swedish chemist Axel Fredrik Cronstedt.
  • The name "nickel" comes from the German word "kupfernickel" meaning "devil's copper".
  • Nickel is the 5th most common element on Earth, making up about 3% of the Earth's core.
  • It is highly resistant to corrosion and oxidation, even at high temperatures.
  • About 65% of all nickel produced is used to make stainless steel.
  • Nickel is magnetic at room temperature, one of only four elements that are.
  • The largest producers of nickel are Indonesia, Philippines, and Russia.
  • Nickel is essential for electric vehicle batteries, particularly in nickel-manganese-cobalt (NMC) batteries.
  • The U.S. five-cent coin (nickel) is actually 75% copper and only 25% nickel.
  • Nickel has a high melting point of 1,455°C (2,651°F).
  • Some people are allergic to nickel, which can cause skin irritation from jewelry or coins.
  • Nickel can be recycled indefinitely without losing its quality or properties.

Nickel Price Analysis (2015-2025)

Major Trends

  • Historic Bubble Cycle: Extraordinary volatility with explosive 2021-2022 rally followed by catastrophic collapse.
  • Post-Bubble Consolidation: Prices stabilized around $15,000 after 50% decline from 2022 peaks.
  • Extreme Volatility Regime: Standard deviations of 30-40% annualized during peak periods.
  • Overall Performance: +53% increase from $10,025 (2015) to $15,288 (2025).

Price Anomalies

  • April 2022 Record High: $31,864 all-time peak driven by EV battery hype and massive speculative positioning.
  • 2023 Catastrophic Collapse: -50% crash from $30,259 as Indonesian supply growth overwhelmed demand.
  • March 2020 COVID Low: $11,484 bottom due to industrial shutdowns and demand destruction.
  • LME Short Squeeze: March 2022 historic squeeze event exacerbated price extremes and market dislocation.
  • Recent Stabilization: $14,900-$17,500 trading range established in 2024-2025 indicating base formation.

Potential Causes

  • EV Battery Demand Hype: Massive speculative positioning around nickel's role in lithium-ion batteries drove bubble conditions.
  • Indonesian Supply Surge: Massive production increases from Indonesia overwhelmed market fundamentals and crashed prices.
  • Stainless Steel Dominance: Approximately 70% of demand from stainless sector creating cyclical industrial exposure.
  • Technological Production Shifts: HPAL vs RKEF production methods affecting cost structures and supply responsiveness.
  • Investor Sentiment Extremes: Retail and institutional frenzy during 2021-2022 followed by complete sentiment reversal.

Future Outlook

  • Extended Basing Period: Likely requires years to fully recover investor confidence after catastrophic bubble collapse.
  • Supply Overhang: Indonesian production dominance creating persistent downward pressure on prices.
  • Critical Support Level: $15,000 represents key stabilization zone for medium-term price direction.
  • Reduced Speculative Interest: Investor appetite likely permanently damaged following massive losses.
  • Gradual Fundamental Recovery: Slow demand growth from legitimate EV applications may eventually absorb excess supply.
