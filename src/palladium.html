Palladium Facts

  • Palladium was discovered in 1803 by English chemist William Hyde Wollaston.
  • It is named after the asteroid Pallas, which was discovered around the same time.
  • Palladium is one of the six platinum group metals (PGMs).
  • It is the least dense and has the lowest melting point of the platinum group metals.
  • About 85% of palladium production is used in catalytic converters for vehicles.
  • Palladium can absorb up to 900 times its own volume of hydrogen, making it useful for hydrogen storage.
  • russia and South Africa are the world's largest producers of palladium.
  • Palladium is more expensive than gold and is one of the most valuable precious metals.
  • It is used in dentistry, electronics, jewelry, and groundwater treatment.
  • Palladium doesn't tarnish in air like silver, making it ideal for fine jewelry.
  • The metal is also used in fuel cells to help generate clean energy.
  • Palladium is 30 times rarer than gold, contributing to its high value.

Palladium Price Analysis (2015-2025)

Major Trends

  • Historic Bull Market: Extraordinary 490% surge from 2016 lows to 2021 peak of $2,959.
  • Catastrophic Collapse: Unprecedented 70% decline from 2021 highs to 2024 lows.
  • Complete Cycle Round-Trip: Prices essentially returned to 2018 levels after massive volatility.
  • Overall Performance: +87% increase from $678 (2015) to $1,265 (2025).

Price Anomalies

  • April 2021 Cycle Peak: $2,959 near-all-time-high driven by massive supply deficits and speculative frenzy.
  • 2022-2024 Market Collapse: -70% crash as EV disruption fears and substitution accelerated.
  • 2025 Recovery Attempt: Modest bounce to $1,264 from 2024 lows of $905.
  • Early 2021 Parabolic Moves: Multiple 15-20% monthly surges during peak speculative period.
  • 2023-2024 Sustained Decline: Consistent -15% to -25% monthly moves during market collapse phase.

Potential Causes

  • EV Disruption Threat: Electric vehicle adoption eliminating catalytic converter demand created structural decline.
  • Russian Supply Dominance: Approximately 40% of global production from Russia created geopolitical risk premium.
  • Dieselgate Effect: Post-2015 shift from platinum to palladium in gasoline engines drove initial demand surge.
  • Substitution Acceleration: Automotive manufacturers switching to cheaper platinum as palladium became uneconomic.
  • Inventory Overhang: Years of accumulated surplus requiring extended period to work through markets.

Future Outlook

  • Structural Decline Likely: Fundamental demand deterioration more severe than typical cyclical downturn.
  • Limited Recovery Potential: Critical resistance at $1,400-$1,600 zone likely caps significant rallies.
  • Permanent Demand Loss: Technological disruption from EV adoption suggests peak prices may never return.
  • Extended Basing Pattern: Likely extended period of sideways consolidation between $800-$1,400 range.
  • Investor Exodus: Many market participants likely permanently exited after catastrophic losses.
