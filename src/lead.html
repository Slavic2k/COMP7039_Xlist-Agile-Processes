Lead Facts

  • Lead has been used by humans for over 8,000 years, with the earliest known artifacts dating to 6500 BCE.
  • It is one of the heaviest common metals, with a density of 11.34 g/cm³.
  • Lead is highly malleable and ductile, making it easy to shape and form.
  • The chemical symbol for lead is Pb, from the Latin word "plumbum" meaning "liquid silver".
  • Lead has a very low melting point of 327.5°C (621.5°F) compared to other metals.
  • Ancient Romans used lead extensively for plumbing, which is where the word "plumbing" comes from.
  • Lead is highly resistant to corrosion, which made it valuable for roofing and plumbing.
  • About 85% of all lead produced today is used in lead-acid batteries for vehicles.
  • Lead is toxic to humans and can cause serious health problems, especially in children.
  • The largest producers of lead today are China, Australia, and the United States.
  • Lead is one of the easiest metals to recycle, with a recycling rate of over 99% for car batteries.
  • Lead is an effective shield against radiation and is used in medical and nuclear applications.

Lead Price Analysis (2015-2025)

Major Trends

  • Range-Bound with Cyclical Peaks: Volatile trading within $1,600-$2,600 range throughout the period.
  • Sustained Downtrend: Gradual decline from 2022 highs to current $1,995 level.
  • Mature Market Characteristics: Limited growth compared to other base metals with established trading patterns.
  • Overall Performance: +18% increase from $1,695 (2015) to $1,995 (2025).

Price Anomalies

  • January 2018 Peak: $2,612 all-time high driven by supply concerns and strong industrial demand.
  • March 2022 Secondary High: $2,416 peak during post-COVID recovery and supply chain disruptions.
  • April 2020 COVID Crash: $1,635 low due to global industrial shutdowns and demand collapse.
  • June 2016 Low: $1,788 bottom caused by global growth concerns and oversupply conditions.
  • Recent Compression: Reduced volatility and tighter trading range in 2024-2025 indicating market maturity.

Potential Causes

  • Recycling Dominance: Approximately 80% of lead comes from recycling, creating natural price stability.
  • Battery Demand Stability: Lead-acid batteries maintain steady demand in automotive and backup power applications.
  • Environmental Regulations: Increasing restrictions affecting primary production and creating supply constraints.
  • Substitution Risk: Lithium-ion batteries replacing some lead-acid applications, limiting growth potential.
  • Industrial Slowdown: Global manufacturing fluctuations directly impacting lead consumption patterns.

Future Outlook

  • Range Persistence: Continued trading within established $1,600-$2,600 band likely given mature market structure.
  • Limited Breakout Potential: Recycling supply dynamics and substitution risks cap significant price appreciation.
  • Volatility Decline: Market maturation suggests continued reduction in price swings over time.
  • Gradual Drift Lower: Potential slow decline if battery substitution accelerates and industrial demand softens.
  • Critical Support Test: Current $1,900-$2,000 zone represents key level for medium-term direction.
