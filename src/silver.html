Silver Facts

  • Silver has been used by humans for over 5,000 years, with the earliest known silver artifacts dating to 4000 BCE.
  • It is the best conductor of electricity of all elements, even better than copper.
  • Silver is the most reflective metal, reflecting about 95% of the visible light spectrum.
  • The chemical symbol for silver is Ag, from the Latin word "argentum" meaning "shiny" or "white".
  • Silver has natural antibacterial properties and was used historically to prevent infection.
  • It is more abundant than gold, with a ratio of about 17.5:1 in the Earth's crust.
  • Mexico is the world's largest producer of silver, followed by Peru and China.
  • Silver is used in photography, electronics, jewelry, and currency.
  • The term "sterling silver" indicates the metal is 92.5% pure silver and 7.5% other metals.
  • Silver can be hammered into sheets so thin that it becomes transparent.
  • About 30% of silver production comes from recycling, particularly from photographic materials.
  • Silver is used in solar panels, with over 100 million ounces used annually in photovoltaic cells.
