Gold Facts

  • Gold is one of the densest of all metals, with a density of 19.3 g/cm³.
  • It is estimated that all the gold ever mined would form a cube about 21 meters on each side.
  • Gold is extremely ductile; a single ounce can be drawn into a wire 5 miles long.
  • The world's largest gold bar weighs 250 kg (551 lb).
  • Over 90% of the world's gold has been mined since the California Gold Rush in 1848.
  • Gold is chemically inert, which means it doesn't corrode or tarnish.
  • The United States holds the largest official gold reserves in the world.
  • Gold is edible and is sometimes used in fancy foods and drinks.
  • Gold is an excellent conductor of electricity and does not tarnish.
  • The chemical symbol for gold is Au, from the Latin word "aurum" meaning "shining dawn".
  • 24-karat gold is pure gold, while 18-karat gold is 75% gold and 25% other metals.
  • Gold is so malleable that it can be hammered thin enough to become transparent.

Gold Price Analysis (2015-2025)

Major Trends

  • Sustained Bull Market: Steady climb from $1,300 to $2,000+ from 2019-2020
  • Explosive Growth Phase: Gold nearly doubled from $1,800 to $3,817 from 2023-2025
  • Overall Performance: +234% increase from $1,141 (2015) to $3,817 (2025)
  • Price Evolution: Sideways trading (2015-2018), Breakout (2019-2020), Consolidation (2021-2022), Parabolic (2023-2025)

Price Anomalies

  • July 2020 Surge: +10.3% monthly gain during COVID safe-haven rally
  • March 2025 Breakout: +9.55% monthly gain in recent major upward move
  • April 2025 Continuation: +7.63% follow-through strength
  • Major Declines: November 2015 (-6.67%), June 2021 (-7.02%), February 2021 (-6.57%)
  • Recent Acceleration: 6 consecutive up-months from April-September 2025

Potential Causes

  • Safe-Haven Demand: COVID pandemic uncertainty driving 2020-2021 rally
  • Inflation Hedging: Response to global economic stimulus and currency concerns
  • Central Bank Buying: Increased gold reserves by global financial institutions
  • Geopolitical Tensions: Global conflicts and economic uncertainty supporting prices
  • Monetary Policy: Low interest rate environment and quantitative easing programs

Future Outlook

  • Momentum Persistence: Strong trends historically persist for multiple years
  • Reduced Drawdowns: Recent corrections have been shallower than historical patterns
  • Structural Support: Evolving role as both defensive asset and growth investment
  • Technical Strength: Multiple resistance breaks at $2,000, $2,500, and $3,000 levels
  • Market Transformation: Transition from traditional safe-haven to strategic portfolio asset
